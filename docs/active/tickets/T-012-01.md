## Research: CTA Copy for Emotional/Reflective Apps

### Principles (from UX literature on emotional product copy)

1. **First-person implied, not second-person imperative**
   - "Select the Lens" tells the user what to do
   - "Shift my perspective" mirrors the user's internal voice
   - "See it differently" speaks to the outcome, not the action

2. **Specificity beats generics**
   - "Ask Lincoln" is more compelling than "Choose a historical figure"
   - "Hear from Dolly Parton" creates anticipation, not just action

3. **Benefit-first microcopy**
   - Bad: "Create Account" — procedural, cold
   - Better: "Save your shifts" — names what you get
   - Best: "Unlock your journal" — names the reward

4. **Friction words to avoid in emotional context**
   - "Select", "Submit", "Confirm", "Choose" — mechanical
   - Replace with: "Hear", "See", "Try", "Explore", "Keep"

5. **Error messages should feel human**
   - Bad: "Please enter text"
   - Good: "Write something first — even a few words is enough"

6. **Gate/paywall copy should not shame**
   - Bad: "You've used your 3 free perspectives"
   - Good: "You've had 3 perspectives today — come back tomorrow or save them all with an account"

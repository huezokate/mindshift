## Vision

Replace the current linear "mind map" screen (a vertical list of boxes with arrows) with a true spatial canvas mind map — the design shown in the Figma lo-fi.

The canvas is the centerpiece of MindShift. When a user completes the onboarding questions, instead of seeing a list, they land on a zoomable canvas with:

- A central "In 5 years..." hub node displaying the user's vision and life categories
- 7 organic-shaped satellite nodes orbiting the center, one per life category (career, creativity, health & wellness, relationships, travel, finances, living situation)
- Curved SVG arrows connecting each satellite to the center
- Each node expandable to show goal bullet points + mood board images
- A "Choose Your Lens" button that overlays a persona's perspective on the map

## Why This Matters

The lo-fi in Figma is not just a redesign — it's a shift from a form output to a vision board. The user should feel like they're looking at their future, not a survey result. The organic shapes, mood images, and spatial layout make it feel personal and alive.
